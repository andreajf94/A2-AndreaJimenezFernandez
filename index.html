Intoduction

Housing policies in Greater Boston have shaped who gets to live where for generations. In the past, practices like redlining, restrictive covenants, and discriminatory lending were used to separate communities by race and income. Even though these practices are no longer legal, their effects can still be seen today in who has access to stable, affordable housing and which neighborhoods remain exclusive or segregated.This analysis is just the beginning of a deeper dive into the lasting impact of historical housing discrimination. By looking at census data and income patterns, we aim to uncover how past policies still shape present-day housing access.
